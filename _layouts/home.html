About Me

I am primarily interested in using genomic and transcriptomic data to answer evolutionary questions with conservation and management implications. Before arriving at Purdue in August 2015, I completed an MS in Biology at Central Michigan University where I studied Antarctic sea spider phylogeography.

In January 2021, I will begin working as an NSF Postdoctoral Fellow in Dr. Janna Willoughby's lab at Auburn University. This work will be a detour from fishes to investigate genomic and environmental determinants of fitness in a fully pedigreed population of banner-tailed kangaroo rats.

Outside of work, I enjoy canoeing, trying to catch as many fish species as I can, and spending time with my spouse and dogs.
